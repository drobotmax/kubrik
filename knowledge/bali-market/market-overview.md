# Bali Real Estate Market Overview

## Tourism & Economy (2025-2026)

- **Tourist arrivals 2025:** 5.47 million (+251% growth trajectory)
- **Indonesia target 2026:** 14-17 million international tourists
- **Bali GDP growth:** ~10% regional
- **Key driver:** Post-pandemic recovery, digital nomad influx, emerging as alternative to Dubai

## Market Dynamics

### Market Scale & Context (sourced: 23 video transcripts)
- Total Bali real estate volume = just 2-3 Dubai high-rises worth of units (tiny market)
- Tourism zone = only 0.6% of the island
- Coastal build-out: Kuta 100%, Seminyak 100%, Canggu 90%
- Max building height: 15 meters (regulation)
- Market NOT overheated in general, but overheated by marketing (bloggers, paid ads, hype)
- Professional investors and family offices starting to enter - demand predictability and legal clarity
- Market consolidating: will remain ~10 serious developers (from current 50+)

### 2026 Market Dynamics (transcript-sourced: QOKqoXKaCeQ + 23 transcripts)
- Rental yields dropped 18% YoY
- Property listings on platforms grew +27%
- Tourist arrivals +9% but spending per tourist declining
- Market maturing: "дилетанты уходят" (amateurs leaving)
- Fewer impulse buyers, more due diligence from investors
- Investor sentiment shifted from "buy anything = profit" to careful selection
- Iran conflict impact: Middle Eastern tourist flow disrupted, Russians unaffected
- Construction cost benchmark: $1,500/m2 basic, $3,000/m2 all-in for experienced devs, up to $4,000/m2 fully legal
- Risk/return ratio IMPROVED vs 1 year ago (risks down, returns stable)

### Supply Pipeline
- **Canggu alone:** 7,100 new 1BR units in pipeline + 568 hotel units (Marriott, Kempinski by 2027)
- **ADR in Canggu** falling 2nd consecutive year - oversaturation signal
- **PBG queue:** grew from 10 to 300 projects (permitting bottleneck)
- **Off-plan sales ban** possible for projects without full permits
- **Many developers building without PBG** - major red flag for buyers

### Demand Drivers
- Russian-speaking investors (primary audience for most developers)
- European buyers (growing, especially for premium/wellness concepts)
- Digital nomads driving rental demand (especially Canggu, Ubud)
- Infrastructure development (new roads, airport expansion plans)
- New airport on North Bali - potential catalyst, but 5+ year horizon

### Supply Landscape
- **Boom in new development:** dozens of new complexes announced 2024-2026
- **Shift toward managed properties:** hotel operators (Wyndham, etc.) increasingly attached to projects
- **Nuanu Creative City:** major new development hub (~44 ha), attracting multiple developers
- **Popular development areas:** Canggu (saturating), Uluwatu/Bukit (growing), Nusa Dua (established), Ubud (niche)
- **Oversaturation signals:** listings growing faster than demand (+27% vs +9% tourists)
- **Developer overextension:** multiple developers stretched thin (Bali Baza: 500+ units, Loyo & Bondar: 1000+ units)
- **Price drops observed:** Alex Villas Complex 7 dropped 20%, Nexa Umalas dropped 28% ($290K to $209K)

### Developer Landscape (updated from transcripts)
- **Tax burden on developers:** 21% (many previously evaded, now enforced)
- **Construction cost tiers:** $1,500/m2 basic | $3,000/m2 all-in experienced | $4,000/m2 fully legal
- **Consolidation:** market moving from 50+ developers to ~10 serious players
- **PBG bottleneck:** queue grew from 10 to 300 projects - slows new supply
- **Off-plan risk:** possible ban on sales without full permits

### Risks & Concerns
- **Oversupply risk** in popular areas (Canggu especially - 7,100 new 1BR units)
- **Airbnb regulation tightening** (see [regulations.md](regulations.md))
- **Leasehold uncertainty** - extension not legally guaranteed
- **Construction quality variance** - ranges from ISO-certified to informal
- **Geopolitical factors** - Middle East tensions affecting tourism flows
- **Currency risk** - IDR fluctuations vs USD pricing
- **Trust crisis** - Domogatsky scandal (19.6K views), SWOI frozen project, PARQ Ubud - eroding investor confidence
- **Investor caution** - "Не вкладывайте больше чем готовы потерять" (don't invest more than you can afford to lose) (src: rTSUVF4FE7E, tZkRtiuqETI)
- **Thailand as alternative** - increasingly compared by investors (easier legal framework, lower returns)
- **Rental yield compression** - 18% YoY drop in yields across popular areas
- **Developers without PBG** - building illegally, buyer assumes risk

### Investor Sentiment (from transcripts)
- Scandals hurt market: Domogatsky (19.6K views), SWOI frozen project, PARQ Ubud
- But: risk/return ratio IMPROVED vs 1 year ago (risks decreased, returns stable)
- Thailand increasingly compared (easier legally, lower returns)
- Professional investors and family offices entering - want predictability, not hype
- New airport on North Bali - potential long-term catalyst (5+ year horizon)

## Market Segments

### Investment Properties (Hotel-managed)
- Apartments/suites with guaranteed management (Wyndham, OXO, etc.)
- Entry: $112K-$260K
- Target ROI: 9.5-20% (developer projections)
- Typical: 30-90m2, leasehold 25-35 years

### Lifestyle Villas
- Standalone or complex villas for personal use + rental income
- Entry: $150K-$700K+
- Typical: 100-300m2 building, 100-600m2 land
- Management via agencies or self-managed

### Land Plots
- Raw land for development
- Prices: 7-11M IDR per are/year (leasehold), varies hugely by location
- Requires PBG (building permit), zoning check

### Townhouses / Co-living
- Growing segment, targets digital nomads and small investors
- Entry: $185K-$330K
- Typical: 70-140m2, private pool, shared infrastructure

## Key Metrics for Marketing

When creating marketing content, reference these data points:
- "5.47 million tourists visited Bali in 2025"
- "Entry from $112,000 for hotel-managed apartments"
- "Leasehold 25-35 years with extension options"
- "Bali GDP growing at ~10%"
- "250m from beach" (for Surfside), "80m from beach" (OceaniQ) - proximity matters
- "Construction cost $1,500-$4,000/m2" - range depends on legality and quality

### Yield Metrics (updated from transcripts)
- **Realistic first-year yield:** 11-12%
- **Established property yield:** up to 14%
- **15%+ practically impossible now** - treat as red flag in developer claims
- **World Bubble Index benchmark:** <8% rental yield = overheated market
- **Bali currently at 10-12%** = healthy by global standards
- "ROI 9.5-20% annually on managed properties" - developer projections, use with disclaimer
- "Rental yields -18% YoY" - use cautiously, validates honest positioning
- "Listings +27% vs tourists +9%" - supply outpacing demand signal
- "Price corrections happening" - Alex Villas -20%, Nexa -28% - validates market cooling narrative

### Nuanu as Market Indicator
- 3,000-5,000 daily visitors (organic, no paid marketing)
- 40 facilities functioning
- First private electric city in Indonesia
- May represent future development model for Bali
- Signals shift toward integrated lifestyle communities vs standalone villas
