# Bali Real Estate Regulations

## Ownership Types

### Hak Sewa (Leasehold)
- **What:** Right to use land/property for a fixed term via notarial agreement
- **Typical terms:** 25-35 years initial + extension option (10-30 years)
- **Best offers:** 30+30 years or 90 years (SDG Sunny Samudra)
- **For foreigners:** Most common and cheapest legal option
- **Registration:** NOT registered at BPN (land office) - only a notarial agreement
- **Extension risk:** Landowner controls extension - this is the KEY RISK. Not legally guaranteed.
- **Key clause to check:** "Guaranteed right of extension at fixed price" (see Blackpoint Ubud listing: 3M IDR/year for full plot)
- **Inheritance:** No formal inheritance mechanism - depends on contract terms
- **Bottom line:** Cheapest option but least protected (Source: Mityuhin/Pronin interview)

### HGB (Hak Guna Bangunan) via PT PMA
- **What:** Right to build on land, held via PT PMA (foreign-owned Indonesian company)
- **Term:** Up to 30 + 20 + 30 = 80 years maximum
- **PMA setup cost:** ~$2,000 initial registration
- **PMA annual maintenance:** $1,700-3,000/year (reporting, compliance, accounting)
- **Registration:** Officially registered at BPN (land office) - strong legal standing
- **Resale advantage:** Cheaper transfer costs compared to Hak Pakai
- **Inheritance:** Can be inherited if company structure is maintained
- **Remote processing:** Can be arranged remotely without being in Indonesia
- **Example:** BIOM freehold villas ($450K-$740K) use HGB structure
- **Premium:** ~30-50% above leasehold equivalent
- (Source: Mityuhin/Pronin interview)

### Hak Pakai (Right to Use)
- **What:** Right to use land, available to foreign individuals (no company required)
- **Term:** Up to 25 + 20 + 25 = 70 years maximum
- **Registration cost:** 13-15M IDR per sotka (100m2)
- **Registration:** Officially registered at BPN (land office)
- **Inheritance:** Can be inherited
- **Remote processing:** Can be arranged remotely
- **Advantage:** Simpler than HGB - no company setup/maintenance needed
- **Disadvantage:** Slightly shorter total term (70 vs 80 years), higher transfer costs on resale
- (Source: Mityuhin/Pronin interview)

### Freehold via Indonesian Nominee
- **What:** Property held in Indonesian citizen's name with side agreements
- **Risk:** HIGH - nominee can legally claim ownership, courts may not enforce side agreements
- **Status:** Technically legal but carries significant risk

### Ownership Comparison Table

| Feature | Hak Sewa (Leasehold) | HGB (via PT PMA) | Hak Pakai |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Max term** | Contract-based (typically 25-35 yrs) | 80 years (30+20+30) | 70 years (25+20+25) |
| **Who can hold** | Foreigners directly | PT PMA (foreign company) | Foreign individuals |
| **BPN registration** | No (notarial only) | Yes | Yes |
| **Setup cost** | Lowest (notary fees) | ~$2,000 PMA + notary | 13-15M IDR/sotka registration |
| **Annual cost** | None (beyond rent) | $1,700-3,000/yr PMA maintenance | Minimal |
| **Inheritance** | Contract-dependent | Yes (via company) | Yes |
| **Extension control** | Landowner decides | Government process | Government process |
| **Resale transfer cost** | Low | Lower than Hak Pakai | Higher than HGB |
| **Remote processing** | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| **Legal protection** | Weakest | Strong | Strong |

### CRITICAL WARNING: "90-Year Freehold" Claims

> **What developers sell as "90-year freehold" is actually HGB split into 30+30+30 year terms.**
>
> - Only the first 30-year term is legally guaranteed
> - Each subsequent extension requires a separate application to BPN
> - There is NO legal mechanism to guarantee extensions decades in advance
> - Investors should understand they are buying 30 years with HOPE of extension
> - Any developer claiming "guaranteed 90-year freehold" is misleading buyers
>
> (Source: Mityuhin/Pronin interview)

### Land Zoning Categories
- **Pink (Pariwisata):** Tourism zone - for villas, hotels, resorts. Most common for investment properties. Tourism zone = 0.6% of island area
- **Yellow:** Residential/mixed use - for housing, some commercial
- **Green:** Agricultural - cannot build without rezoning. **Green zones cannot be re-zoned anymore**
- **Max building height:** 15 meters
- **Important:** Always verify zoning before purchase

## Airbnb & Short-term Rental Regulation (2025-2026)

### Timeline
- **December 2025:** Governor Wayan Koster proposed Airbnb ban on Bali
- **Reason:** 2,000+ unlicensed properties, tax evasion, unfair competition with hotels
- **Ministry response:** Full ban NOT supported (Indonesia targets 14-17M tourists in 2026)

### Current Status (March 2026)
- **Deadline: March 31, 2026** - all properties on Airbnb/Booking must confirm licenses
- **Required:** NIB (Business Identification Number) + OSS (Online Single Submission) registration
- **Consequence:** Unlicensed properties removed from platform search results (not completely blocked)
- **Benefit for compliant properties:** "Registered and Licensed" badge, competitive advantage

### Enforcement Reality
- **Enforcement is patchy** - not uniformly applied across Bali
- Properties without NIB are being removed from search results, but not fully blocked from platforms
- NIB registration cost is relatively low but bureaucratic
- Hotel-managed properties (Wyndham, OXO) are inherently compliant - no extra steps needed
- Self-managed properties must register individually through OSS
- (Source: Mityuhin/Pronin interview, QOKqoXKaCeQ transcript)

### Impact on Investors
- **Positive:** Clears out amateur/illegal operators, benefits professional management
- **Negative:** More bureaucracy, compliance costs, potential rental income disruption during transition
- **Marketing angle:** "Licensed and professionally managed" becomes a selling point
- **Key insight:** Hotel-managed properties (Wyndham, OXO) are inherently compliant - zero hassle for investor

## Building Permits

### PBG (Persetujuan Bangunan Gedung)
- **Required for:** Any new construction
- **Process:** Application via OSS system, includes environmental review
- **Timeline:** 2-6 months depending on complexity
- **SLF (Sertifikat Laik Fungsi):** Building worthiness certificate - required before occupancy
- **AMDAL (Environmental Impact Assessment):** Required for large projects; some developers skip it for smaller ones
- **Due diligence:** Always request PBG + SLF documentation from developers

### RED FLAG: Construction Without PBG
- Some developers start construction WITHOUT obtaining PBG first
- This is a major risk - property may face legal issues, demolition orders, or inability to get SLF
- **Always verify PBG exists before investing** - request the document and verify its authenticity
- Construction start without PBG: still happening but increasingly risky
- **PBG queue:** from 10 to 300 projects waiting - massive backlog
- **SLF path** (self-certify after construction) no longer works after PARQ case
- **Off-plan sales ban planned** - selling without all permits may be prohibited
- (Source: tZkRtiuqETI transcript)

## Tax & Financial Considerations

### Property Tax
- **PBB (annual):** 0.5% of assessed value (NJOP)

### Property Sale Taxes
- **Seller tax (PPh):** 2.5% of transaction value
- **Buyer tax (BPHTB):** 5% of transaction value
- **Total transaction cost:** 7.5% split between buyer and seller
- **Total developer tax burden:** 21% (transfer tax + VAT 11%) - many developers previously didn't pay

### Rental Income Tax
- **Indonesian tax residents:** 10% of rental income
- **Non-residents (most foreign investors):** 20% withholding tax on rental income
- **Via PT PMA:** Standard corporate tax rates may apply (can be more favorable depending on structure)
- (Source: Mityuhin/Pronin interview)

## Due Diligence Checklist

When evaluating properties, verify ALL of the following:

### Legal & Title
1. Land certificate type (SHM, HGB, Hak Pakai) - understand what you're actually buying
2. BPN registration confirmation (for HGB/Hak Pakai)
3. Zoning verification (Pink/Yellow/Green) - matches intended use
4. Verify "freehold" claims - ask: is it HGB 30+30+30? What guarantees extensions?
5. If leasehold: review extension clause, who controls it, at what price

### Permits & Compliance
6. PBG (building permit) - **verify it exists BEFORE construction, not after**
7. SLF (building worthiness certificate) - required before occupancy
8. IMB/NIB (business license for rental activity)
9. AMDAL/UKL-UPL (environmental permits) - required for larger projects
10. Airbnb/OTA compliance - NIB registration for short-term rental

### Corporate & Financial
11. Developer company registration (PT) - verify it's active and in good standing
12. If buying via PMA: understand annual maintenance costs ($1,700-3,000/yr)
13. Tax structure clarity - 10% vs 20% rental tax depending on residency status
14. Insurance (construction + property)
15. Management agreement (if hotel-managed)

### Red Flags to Watch For
- Developer claims "90-year freehold" without explaining HGB extension process
- Construction started without PBG
- No BPN registration for HGB/Hak Pakai property
- Developer avoids showing permit documentation
- Leasehold with no clear extension mechanism
