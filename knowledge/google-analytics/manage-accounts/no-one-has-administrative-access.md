If you are the owner of the Google Analytics account or one of the administrators who manages the Google Analytics account, you need to prove your ownership of the sites and/or apps that are sending data to Google Analytics.

If there are Google Ads and/or AdSense accounts linked to the Google Analytics account, you also need to prove your ownership of those accounts.

Once you've proven your ownership of the sites and/or apps, fill out and submit the [form](https://support.google.com/analytics/contact/7303233).

## Prove ownership of the sites and/or apps

Be sure you review the [permissions you'd like to be granted](https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/2884495).

**Note**: Recovery for Google Tag Manager or Google Analytics for Firebase is currently not available.

### Prove site ownership

Make sure you follow the format and the wording exactly as provided. Incomplete and/or incorrect wording will prevent us from proceeding with your request.

1.   Create an `analytics.txt` file with the following information and make sure you enter the [measurement ID](https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/12270356): `GooGhywoiu9839t543j0s7543uw1 - pls add {INSERT EMAIL ADDRESS} to GA account {INSERT MEASUREMENT ID} with ‘Administrator’ permissions - date {INSERT DATE}.` 

**Note**:

*   If you cannot prove ownership of all the sites identified by Website URLs in the Analytics account, then we cannot grant access.
*   To verify ownership of your account, you must provide the measurement ID for every property in the account or the account ID. You can do this by doing one of the following: 
    *   Including a separate line for each measurement ID
    *   Including all the measurement IDs on one line, separate by commas
    *   Providing the account ID on one line

1.   Add the `analytics.txt` file to the top-level domain, for example, `example.com/analytics.txt`. If the URL of the site that is sending data to Analytics is a subdomain, then you can upload `analytics.txt` to either the subdomain or the top-level domain, for example: `sales.example.com/analytics.txt or example.com/analytics.txt`

2.   If there are Google Ads and/or AdSense accounts linked to the Analytics account, you need to [prove your ownership of those accounts](https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/15191460?hl=en#prove), as well.
3.   Fill out the Google Analytics [Contact us](https://support.google.com/analytics/contact/7303233) form.

**Note**:

*   If you cannot prove ownership of all the sites identified by [Website URLs](https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/9304776#edit-data-stream) in the Google Analytics account, then we cannot grant access.
*   If your website does not allow uploading files, then add a meta tag with the following information to the page that is identified as the [Website URLs in Google Analytics](https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/9304776#edit-data-stream):

`GooGhywoiu9839t543j0s7543uw1 - pls add {INSERT EMAIL ADDRESS} to GA account {INSERT MEASUREMENT ID} with ‘Administrator‘ permissions - date {INSERT DATE}.`

## [](https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/15191460?hl=en)Prove ownership of linked Google Ads and/or AdSense accounts

If there are Google Ads and/or AdSense accounts linked to the Analytics account, you need to provide the following information for those accounts:

*   Payment information 
    *   **Credit card or direct debit**: provide the exact amount of the last charge and the date on which it was made.
    *   **Invoicing**: provide the exact amount and date of your last invoice.
    *   **Bank transfer**: provide the reference number and amount of your last transfer.

*   Payment-instrument details 
    *   **Credit card**: provide the type of credit card (MasterCard, VISA, Discover, etc.), your name as it appears on the card, the last 4 digits of the card number, and the expiration date. Do not send your complete card number.
    *   **Direct debit**: provide the last four digits of your bank-account number.

*   Company name and the billing address listed in your Google Ads account.
*   The display URL (web address that appears in your ads) of the website you are advertising.
*   Sample keywords for one or more of your campaigns

**Note**: You can submit the account-payment information, along with the other required information from the form link at the end of this article.

## Fill out and submit form

_After_ you have uploaded the `analytics.txt` file, then fill out and submit the following [form](https://support.google.com/analytics/contact/7303233), and our support team will contact you.
