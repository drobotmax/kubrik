## [](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/14261098?hl=en)How it works

After you’ve set up your [Merchant Center account](https://merchants.google.com/) and have approved products, you’ll automatically be enrolled in this program. Your progress will be monitored automatically, and [benefits](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/14261098?hl=en#benefits) will be applied to your offers when you meet a certain performance threshold.

The Store Quality Score is calculated at domain level. In [CSS program countries](https://support.google.com/merchants/topic/12652782), if you manage multiple Merchant Center accounts, the score will only appear in the Merchant Center account used to participate in [Free Listings](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/13889434) that had active organic offers in the past 30 days. The score will not be visible across all your Merchant Center accounts.

This program will monitor the experience you provide in several areas, including:

*   [Shipping Experience](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/14261098?hl=en#shipping)
*   [Return Experience](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/14261098?hl=en#return)
*   [Browsing Experience](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/14261098?hl=en#browsing)
*   [Purchase Experience](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/14261098?hl=en#purchase)

For example, your Shipping category score will contain metric scores for your delivery time and shipping cost. For each metric in each category, you’ll be given a rating of “Low”, “Fair” , “Good”, “Great”, or “Exceptional” that will also determine your overall quality score. To earn the “Top Quality Store” badge, you must rank “Exceptional” in your overall quality score. Learn more about [Top Quality Stores](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/14286226).

**Note**: You don’t need to rank “Exceptional” in each category to rank “Exceptional” in your overall quality score.

The program is meant to reward merchants who are providing an excellent customer experience. You can benefit from this program regardless of the size or industry of your business. You won't be penalized if you don’t offer a certain level of customer experience or if you don’t provide some or all of the requested information. You just won't receive the [benefits](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/14261098?hl=en#benefits) listed above.

### [How metrics are calculated](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/14261098?hl=en)

The program metric values don’t display directly to customers on an individual offer level. Instead, the program metrics are impression-weighted averages and are only displayed in your [Merchant Center account](https://merchants.google.com/mc/quality).

Google will evaluate the information that you provide each day. Even though all metrics are calculated based on an impression-weighted average, there's a fixed lookback period. So, if your rating has been in “Opportunity” or “Comparable” for a while, it may take a few weeks for your improvements to be reflected in Google's evaluation. All merchants on the platform are eligible to receive benefits from the program, and your performance is evaluated against all other merchants.

**Note**: The standards and criteria for earning the "Top Quality Store" badge will be updated regularly to provide an exceptional shopping experience. These changes could result in gaining or losing the "Top Quality Store" badge, independent of the quality of the signals provided. For the most updated information regarding your badge status and signal quality, visit the "Store Quality" page in your [Merchant Center account](https://merchants.google.com/mc/quality).

**Keep in mind**: If you don’t have any ratings or if you’re missing specific ratings, this could either be because you’re not currently providing the required information or because your offers haven’t received sufficient impressions in the lookback period.

Also, if you’re providing offer-level shipping data, make sure to include both shipping cost and [transit time](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/6324484#minmaxtransit) for each offer. Your offer-level data will override your account-level data, so the Store Quality feature will use these values when determining your performance level.

Check out the ["Store Quality" page](https://merchants.google.com/mc/quality) to view the areas where you’re providing information and to learn how you can update missing information.

**Note**: You can manage shipping and return signals using Store Quality in Search Console, provided your Merchant Center account is linked to your Search Console property. For instructions on associating your Merchant Center account with a Search Console property, review the "Request a Merchant Center account association" section in the [Search Console Help article](https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/9419894#zippy=%2Crequest-an-association%2Crequest-a-merchant-center-account-association).

#### Example

Barbara’s Buckles has a blank rating for shipping speed. After a quick check, they realize that they’re providing shipping costs without providing shipping speed. So, they create a shipping service for their products that includes both shipping costs and shipping speed. After the lookback window passes, their rating updates with a new value to reflect their shipping speed, which may boost their visibility and performance on the "Shopping" tab.

### [How to view your performance](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/14261098?hl=en)

By default, your “Store Quality” page will show how you compare to other merchants that sell within your category. If you sell across multiple categories, it will default to show how you compare against all stores.

In CSS program countries, if you manage multiple Merchant Center accounts, the score will only be visible in the Merchant Center used for Free Listings.

To view your performance for each metric and your overall score in Merchant Center Next:

![Image 2: Step 1](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/Lqn87u8yoXab3NvtMYC58WwYvAWuz065pCoR) In your [Merchant Center account](https://merchants.google.com/), click **Store Quality** under the “Products & store” section in the left navigation menu.

![Image 3: Step 2](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/sXXE8ayMzITWqMQhoFgSh1dUY1YFF37QlfLJ) To view how you compare to direct competitors, click the **Compare to** button then select **Direct competitors**.

*   **Note**: Your direct competitors are those stores who show products next to your products.

To earn a badge, you must perform well relative to other merchants in all the required metrics. All metrics are calculated daily and have a lookback period to ensure continuous performance.

### [How to improve your performance](https://support.google.com/merchants/answer/14261098?hl=en)

#### Store Rating

A rating calibrated by Google based on multiple sources, including Google Customer Reviews, reviews from various independent rating websites, and performance metrics from Google-led shopping research.

To opt out of the Store Quality program, contact support via the 'Contact us' button below. Bear in mind that there are no program penalties for not meeting a certain performance standard. This program is only meant to reward retailers who provide an excellent customer experience.
